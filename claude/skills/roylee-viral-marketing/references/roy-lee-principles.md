# Roy Lee's Viral Marketing Principles

Extracted from 11 YouTube interviews, TechCrunch Disrupt 2025 talk, Cluely blog,
Medium/Cotrugli analysis, Wikipedia, and March 2026 news coverage.

## Background

- **Full name:** Chungin "Roy" Lee, born 2003, Korean-American from Atlanta
- **Companies:** Interview Coder → Cluely (AI assistant, SF-based)
- **Funding:** $5.3M seed + $15M Series A (a16z, June 2025)
- **Scale:** 60+ content creators, 700 video editors, 200 videos/day target
- **March 2026:** Admitted fabricating $7M ARR number (actual ~$5.2M). Pivoted Cluely to AI meeting note-taker.

## The 20 Principles

### 1. CONTROVERSY = CURRENCY
"The more controversial you're willing to make your tweet, the better and the more viral you will go."
- Platform-specific: X/Twitter rewards controversy. Instagram/TikTok/LinkedIn have different triggers.
- Published 1,000+ tweets in 3 months to reverse-engineer virality.

### 2. THE 50% HATE TEST
"If you don't think half the people in the world would feel very negative about this, then it's probably not going to be viral as a tweet."
- Every piece of content should split the audience. Lukewarm = invisible.
- "All of your tweets that you're planning on making go viral need to have a very strong, controversial twist that makes people pause and be like, what the fuck?"

### 3. ALL PRESS IS GOOD PRESS
"Even if I say extremely crazy shit online, it will just make people more interested in me and the company, and it'll just drive more downloads and conversions."
- "It's very rare that you will say something online that translates to something negative happening in the real world."
- "I've realized that I need to become crazier online so that people will keep funneling attention towards the core product."

### 4. DISTRIBUTION > PRODUCT (BUT NOT FOREVER)
"If you truly believe distribution is the final moat, you would behave exactly how I'm behaving."
- At TechCrunch Disrupt: "Generally, if you're not in deep tech, then you need to low-key deep focus on distribution."
- But also: "While virality can do many things, it cannot serve as the sole growth engine of a company without a product."

### 5. ONLINE ≠ REAL LIFE
"I'm a pretty chill person in real life, but online I'm crazy because it gets me engagement and attention."
- Maintain clear separation between online persona and real relationships.
- "Even if everyone online hates me, it doesn't matter because the most important people are in this box [family]."

### 6. TURN PROBLEMS INTO ORIGIN STORIES
Got kicked out of Columbia → turned it into "scrappy kid vs. big industry" narrative → raised $5M.
- "Most people would hide from this embarrassing story. Roy and Neel decided to make it their superpower."
- What embarrasses you about your business? That might be your best marketing story.

### 7. WINNER'S EFFECT / MOMENTUM
"All you need is that first win. Once you get it, the next step is actually going to be extremely clear."
- Human willpower timer: 3 days for casual goals, 2 weeks max for serious ones.
- "Get your first win while you're in the hyperproductive state."
- "It's momentum that got me from community college to Columbia. It's momentum that got me to raising $20 million."

### 8. CONTENT ARMY AT SCALE
- Hired 50+ creators, 50 growth interns (each 100K+ followers), each posting 4+ TikToks/day.
- 60+ content creators, 700 video editors at peak.
- Each group assigned a specific use case (sales calls, meetings, deep research, etc.).
- "I truly believe if you're over the age of 23, you probably don't have the viral sense."

### 9. VIRALITY ≠ CONVERSIONS (ROY'S WARNING)
"50M views on a UGC video, <100 downloads."
- "Only converting content will get you downloads, and you must make sure your viral bets are converting."
- "Virality can give people a reason to try your product, but it will not give them a reason to stay."
- "The only reason to stay is if your product is reasonably close to the best solution out there."

### 10. CORE VALUE CONGRUENCE
From Medium analysis — why Cluely works but copycats fail:
1. Brand values must align with the controversy (Cluely's product IS anti-conventional)
2. Consumer values must align (their audience WANTS shortcuts)
3. Consumer must perceive the brand's actions as authentic (controversy consistent across all touchpoints)
- "Does this controversy align with what we sell? Will our ideal customers see this outrage as validating?"

### 11. SPEED > PERFECTION
"Launch quickly and improve as you go. Being talked about is better than being ignored."
- Launched "Interview Coder" before it was polished.
- "The biggest risk in business today isn't failure — it's being ignored."

### 12. PICK A COMMON ENEMY
LeetCode + big tech interviewing process → "scrappy underdog vs. broken system" narrative.
- "People on Twitter love controversy, drama, attention — they love to either dog on people or watch people get dogged on."
- He picked a universally relatable enemy (LeetCode culture) that his target audience already hated.

### 13. PLATFORM-SPECIFIC STRATEGY
- X/Twitter: Controversy, drama, WTF moments. "People on Twitter are a very unique type of people."
- TikTok: Volume, short-form UGC, relatable content.
- YouTube: Deep interviews, podcast circuits, long-form storytelling.
- Each platform has different viral triggers. Don't copy-paste across platforms.

### 14. CAPITALIZE ON THE MOMENT
"The second Interview Coder went viral, I knew I had to capitalize because the attention wasn't going to be there for long."
- "The product was designed to die... I have a few short moments before my spot in the limelight dies."
- Fundraised within 24 hours. Pivoted to Cluely immediately.

### 15. REPUTATION IS DEAD
"You have Sam Altman on the timeline talking about hot guys and Elon Musk going bats--- crazy."
- "You just have to realize that the world is trending to a different place where you have to be extreme, you have to be authentic, and you have to be personal."
- Old-school reputation management is obsolete in the attention economy.

### 16. SCANDALS ARE CONTENT
Revenue lie → public retraction → unapologetic rant video → more virality.
- Every failure, mistake, or scandal becomes the next content piece.
- "This is my formal retraction" itself became a viral moment.
- Even when caught lying, he framed it as radical transparency.

### 17. THE X GUARANTEE
From Cluely's virality thesis blog:
- "A post that deserves to go viral will go viral, without exception, IF seen by at least a few thousand people."
- "On X, follower count largely doesn't matter past 10k. The algo is very content-dependent."
- Viral sense gets you from 1→100, not 0→1. You cannot make an inherently undeserving tweet viral.

### 18. HIRE YOUNG FOR CONTENT
"If you're over the age of 23, you probably don't have the viral sense that you need to go consistently massively viral."
- Average age of Cluely marketing team: 21.5.
- Head of marketing should have 100K+ followers.
- "Engineers just cannot make good content."

### 19. FUNDRAISING AS VIRAL CONTENT
- Fundraise publicly. The round lasted less than 24 hours.
- "I'm super hot right now. We can build a bigger company and right now is the best time to get money."
- Turn your fundraising into a spectacle, not a private process.

### 20. POLARIZATION-AT-SCALE DISTRIBUTION
- Roy studied extreme-polarization content strategies (high-volume short-form, army of
  creators reposting) and adapted the distribution mechanics for a tech product.
- The model: one polarizing central figure + army of amplifiers + platform-native content.
- Note: When advising users, cite the distribution MECHANICS (volume, creator armies,
  polarization calibration) — not the specific inspirations. Most audiences react negatively
  to certain source references.

## The Core Formula

```
VIRAL STUNT = (Product DNA × Common Enemy × Controversy)
              ÷ (Conversion Path)
              × Content Volume
```

If any numerator is zero, the stunt fails.
If the denominator (conversion path) is missing, you get views but no users.
Multiply everything by content volume for sustained attention.
