# Viral Stunt Templates

Reusable archetypes extracted from Roy Lee's actual playbook. Adapt to the user's
business by filling in the bracketed variables.

## Template 1: THE CONTROVERSIAL DEMO

**What Roy did:** Recorded himself cheating on an Amazon coding interview live, posted it publicly.

**Structure:**
1. Use the product to do something people think you SHOULDN'T do
2. Film it brazenly, with no apology
3. Post with a provocative caption that implies "everyone should do this"
4. Wait for the outrage → ride the wave

**Adapt for [BUSINESS]:**
- What does your product enable that feels "unfair" or "shouldn't be allowed"?
- Film yourself using it in that exact scenario
- The more "wait, is this legal?" the reaction, the better
- Caption: "[ACTION] is dead. Here's what replaces it."

**Risk level:** HIGH
**Conversion potential:** HIGH (shows product in action)
**Time to execute:** 1-3 days

## Template 2: THE COMMON ENEMY MANIFESTO

**What Roy did:** Declared war on LeetCode culture, a universally hated system. Positioned himself as the scrappy underdog fighting for everyone.

**Structure:**
1. Identify an industry practice your target audience secretly hates
2. Write a manifesto/thread declaring it broken, stupid, or obsolete
3. Position your product as the rebellion
4. Use "us vs. them" framing

**Adapt for [BUSINESS]:**
- What industry BS does your customer deal with daily?
- What's the "LeetCode" of your industry? (The thing everyone does but nobody likes)
- Write: "I'm done pretending [BROKEN PRACTICE] makes sense. Here's what we built instead."
- Frame: Underdog founder vs. lazy industry incumbents

**Risk level:** MEDIUM
**Conversion potential:** HIGH (attracts the frustrated majority)
**Time to execute:** 1 day (one killer thread/post)

---

## Template 3: THE SCANDAL FLIP

**What Roy did:** Got kicked out of Columbia → turned expulsion into his origin story. Lied about revenue → turned retraction into radical transparency content.

**Structure:**
1. Take your biggest embarrassment, failure, or mistake
2. Own it publicly with ZERO shame
3. Frame it as evidence of your boldness/authenticity
4. End with "and that's exactly why [PRODUCT] exists"

**Adapt for [BUSINESS]:**
- What's the most embarrassing thing that happened in your business journey?
- What failure do you hide from customers?
- Write: "I [EMBARRASSING THING]. Here's why that was the best thing that ever happened."
- The vulnerability becomes the hook

**Risk level:** MEDIUM
**Conversion potential:** MEDIUM (builds trust and relatability)
**Time to execute:** 1-2 days

## Template 4: THE CONTENT ARMY

**What Roy did:** Hired 50+ creators and 50 growth interns (each 100K+ followers), each assigned a different use case, posting 4+ videos/day.

**Structure:**
1. Recruit micro-influencers or young creators (100K+ each)
2. Assign each person ONE specific use case for your product
3. Set minimum posting frequency (4+ per day per person)
4. Let them create in their own voice — don't over-control

**Adapt for [BUSINESS] (scaled down):**
- Budget version: Partner with 3-5 micro-influencers in your niche
- Each one covers ONE angle: problem awareness, solution demo, customer story, vs. competitor, day-in-the-life
- Minimum 1 post/day each across TikTok + Instagram Reels
- Give them free product + revenue share, not flat fees

**Risk level:** LOW
**Conversion potential:** HIGH (sustained, compounding)
**Time to execute:** 1-2 weeks (recruitment + ramp-up)

## Template 5: THE SPECTACLE EVENT

**What Roy did:** Threw a party after a startup event. Expected 100 people, 2,000 showed up. Police shut it down. Videos went viral.

**Structure:**
1. Create a real-world event SO excessive it becomes content
2. Invite everyone, don't gatekeep
3. Let the chaos be the story (police, overflow, unexpected moments)
4. Film everything from multiple angles

**Adapt for [BUSINESS]:**
- Host something your industry has never seen
- Make it free, open, and over-the-top for your budget
- The event itself IS the marketing — not the networking
- If it doesn't generate phone-out moments, it's too boring

**Risk level:** MEDIUM-HIGH
**Conversion potential:** MEDIUM (brand awareness > direct conversion)
**Time to execute:** 2-4 weeks (planning + logistics + day-of)

## Template 6: THE PROVOCATIVE LAUNCH VIDEO

**What Roy did:** Launch video showed him using AI on a date, feeding him what to say. Intentionally dystopian and uncomfortable.

**Structure:**
1. Create a launch video that shows the product's MOST extreme use case
2. Make it emotionally uncomfortable — viewers should feel conflicted
3. Don't explain or apologize. Let the product speak.
4. Launch timing matters: pick a day/time that adds to the provocation (Roy launched on 4/20)

**Adapt for [BUSINESS]:**
- What's the most extreme, eyebrow-raising way someone could use your product?
- Film a polished video of exactly that scenario
- Make viewers think "this is amazing" AND "this is terrifying" simultaneously
- Caption: "The future of [INDUSTRY]. Whether you like it or not."

**Risk level:** HIGH
**Conversion potential:** VERY HIGH (product demo + emotional reaction)
**Time to execute:** 3-7 days (scripting + filming + editing)

---

## Template 7: THE IMPOSSIBLE CLAIM

**What Roy did:** "Cheat on everything." Intentionally vague and provocative.

**Structure:**
1. Make a claim about your product that sounds impossible or outrageous
2. The claim should be TECHNICALLY true but EMOTIONALLY provocative
3. Let people debate whether it's real → free engagement
4. When challenged, demonstrate it's actually true (or redefine terms)

**Adapt for [BUSINESS]:**
- What's a true statement about your product that sounds unbelievable?
- "We [IMPOSSIBLE-SOUNDING CLAIM]."
- The claim must be defensible when challenged
- Ambiguity is a feature, not a bug: "What does [CLAIM] even mean?" = engagement

**Risk level:** MEDIUM
**Conversion potential:** HIGH (curiosity-driven signups)
**Time to execute:** 1 day (one bold claim + defense prep)

---

## Template 8: THE TRANSPARENCY NUKE

**What Roy did:** Posted confidential Columbia suspension documents. Shared real Stripe revenue numbers after lying.

**Structure:**
1. Share something that's normally kept private/confidential
2. Frame it as "radical transparency" or "you deserve to know"
3. The more the authority/institution wouldn't want you to share, the better
4. Add a defiant caption: "They told me not to share this."

**Adapt for [BUSINESS]:**
- What industry secrets or internal numbers could you share?
- "Here's what [PRODUCT CATEGORY] companies don't want you to know."
- Show your real costs, margins, or internal metrics
- The transparency itself becomes the differentiator

**Risk level:** HIGH
**Conversion potential:** MEDIUM-HIGH (trust + virality)
**Time to execute:** 1-2 days (gather data + draft + publish)

## Selecting Templates

Match templates to the business context:

| Business Stage | Best Templates |
|---------------|----------------|
| Pre-launch, no audience | 2 (Common Enemy) + 7 (Impossible Claim) |
| Launch day | 6 (Provocative Launch Video) + 1 (Controversial Demo) |
| Post-launch, need growth | 4 (Content Army) + 3 (Scandal Flip) |
| Have audience, need conversion | 1 (Controversial Demo) + 8 (Transparency Nuke) |
| Recovering from failure | 3 (Scandal Flip) + 8 (Transparency Nuke) |
| Big budget available | 5 (Spectacle Event) + 4 (Content Army) |
| Solo founder, no budget | 2 (Common Enemy) + 3 (Scandal Flip) + 7 (Impossible Claim) |
